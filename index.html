Multimodal Representation Learning Based Voice Conversion for Electrolaryngeal Speech Enhancement via Contrastive Learning

Authors: Ding Ma, Jinyi Mi, Jiajun He, Kazuhiro Kobayashi, Naoki Nishio, and Tomoki Toda
Comments: Submitted to APSIPA ASC 2026.

Abstract: Electrolaryngeal (EL) speech suffers from severe acoustic mismatches with normal speech, resulting in degraded naturalness and intelligibility. These mismatches also make sequence-to-sequence voice conversion struggle to learn robust intermediate representations, hindering EL-speech-to-normal-speech conversion (EL2SP) performance. To address this, we propose a novel multimodal contrastive learning framework to 1) achieve richer multimodal-representation learning, and 2) facilitate the transferability of multimodal representations to EL2SP. First, paired EL speech and text representations are fused to learn multimodal representations for target speech generation, during which contrastive learning is employed to encourage a semantically consistent speech–text representation space. Next, the learned fused representations are treated as fixed targets to guide speech-only representation learning through contrastive learning, enabling multimodal knowledge to be transferred to the EL2SP task without increasing model complexity. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method consistently outperforms not only speech-only baseline but also the baseline under the same multimodal input conditions.

Main concept

Dataset

Patient 1 and Patient 2 datasets were used to evaluate experimental systems for EL2SP.

Systems

  • EL speech, Normal speech: Original source EL and target normal speech as the reference.
  • S-VC: Speech representation learning by first conducting pretraining then conducting two-stage fine-tuning with the synthetic data and original EL2SP dataset.
  • MR-VC: Motimodal representation learning method trained with the synthetic data, original EL2SP dataset, and text data.
  • ours-MRCL: Proposed motimodal representation learning method using two-stage cross modal contrastive learning and trained with the synthetic data, original EL2SP dataset, and text data.
